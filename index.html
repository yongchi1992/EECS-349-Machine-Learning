Summary

As our topic is about stock price prediction, we will use history data for our input. The attributes we choose to describe the history data include opening price, closing price, highest price, lowest price and volume. Our algorithm will output the trading strategy for the next day: buying, selling or doing nothing. Our task is important because stock is always a good way to invest.

Report

We are going to use the stock data from 2012-2016. The attributes we choose to describe the history data include opening price, closing price, highest price, lowest price and volume. We are going to use the stock data from 2012-2014 as our training set, stock data in 2015 as our validation set, and data in 2016 as our testing set. We are going to set up a suitable rolling-window training scheme, which means our training set will synchronize with the new date. The length of our window depends on the result of the training data.

Conclusion

Our algorithm performs SVM (Support Vector Machine) on stock price prediction. We plot the Cumulative Rate of Return of our trading strategy as above. As we can see our trading strategy performs a rate of return 35% in 90 days, with a success rate of 53.15%. During our study, we use LIBSVM, which is a library for support vector machines.
